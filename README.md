## Thirteen Lines

> A low green clerk leans out of static, asks for time.  
> He sells a clock whose second hand can bear your weight.  
> You strap in where the wire stars and stomach rhyme.  
> Earth turns below; the Moon keeps orbit, thin and late.  
> A sidereal day is `23:56:04`.  
> Solar noon comes slower, asking four more minutes more.  
> Mercury runs brief; Venus spins backward into flame.  
> Starlink and broken stations stitch their rust around the blue.  
> Bruno burned for plural worlds; Hypatia died for names.  
> Still every heresy of motion circles back as true.  
> Press `B` and loose a green cone through the vacuum black.  
> Let barf inherit all your speed and glitter down the track.  
> Then choose `1`, `2`, or `3`, and ride the clock that won't come back.
