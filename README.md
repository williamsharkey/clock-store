In Clock Store's wire the coughing shopkeep grins,  
And says, buy one strange clock, then straps you to its sweep;  
The second hand spits green confession from your sins,  
While hour and minute drag the planet through its sleep.  
A sidereal day is `23:56:04`,  
Because the stars stand still while solar noon must slide;  
The Sun asks roughly four more minutes at the door,  
Before the noon we name comes lumbering beside.  
Mercury keeps an eighty-eight-day year in flame,  
Yet one full solar day there sprawls through `176`;  
Venus rolls backward, `243` days to name  
One spin, as if the clock itself were built to sicken tricks.  
Jove turns in nine hours, fifty-five minutes or so,  
While Saturn's moons and ring-shards thread a colder throw.  
The Moon backs off by near `3.8 cm` each year,  
And Earth's slow precession needs twenty-six millennia;  
Starlink swarms, the Station, junk, and old machines appear,  
A scrapyard hymn in vectors over bright Oceania.  
Bruno burned in `1600` where plural worlds were aired;  
Hypatia fell in `415` where thought met zeal unkind.  
To chart the sky, ask what is true, and say time's gears are weird,  
Has long been reason enough to make the dogma blind.  
So press `B` and loose the bag in one green, needled cone;  
Let barf chunks keep your velocity and sail alone.  
Choose `1`, `2`, or `3`, let moon and planet hecklers talk;  
Ride Earth by sharp wire hands and laugh until the stars all walk.
